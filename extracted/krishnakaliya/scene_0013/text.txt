Krishna’s dance stopped. He stepped lightly off Kaliya's head. The serpent, humbled and 
terrified, pleaded, "If I leave, the great eagle Garuda will surely eat me!" Krishna smiled 
gently and pointed to Kaliya's hood. There, shining with divine light, were the prints of 
Krishna's small feet. "Fear not," Krishna said. "You have been marked by God. Garuda 
will not harm you now." And so, the poison left the veins of the river, as Kaliya and his 
family departed in peace.
