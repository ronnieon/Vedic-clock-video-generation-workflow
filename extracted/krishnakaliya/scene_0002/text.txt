In the heart of beautiful Vrindavan, a poison bloomed. Where the sacred Yamuna river 
bent, the water turned to black, bubbling sludge. The air itself burned, and any bird that 
flew across fell from the sky. This was the domain of Kaliya, the exiled serpent king. But 
on the branch of a tall Kadamba tree overlooking the blight, a young boy with skin like a 
rain cloud tightened his sash. "You’ve squatted in My river long enough, Uncle," Krishna 
said with a smirk, slapping his bicep with a sound like thunder. "Time to wake up."
